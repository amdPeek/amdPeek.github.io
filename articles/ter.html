Extracting reactions from the Brenda database

In this example, only 5 reactions lead to distance 2 from lactose to resolufin. At distance 3 there are 132 and at distance 4 it is 5770.

The purpose of this project is to extract from a database of enzymatic reactions (BRENDA) those which form a path starting from given reactants and which end in a given product. The set of BRENDA's reactions (approximately 53000) constitutes a directed graph which can have cycles. The goal is to enumerate all related subgraphs (paths of given length) that have inputs and a specified output. The problem is known to be NP-complete and the naive algorithm is exponential in the length of the searched paths.


This was my research project for my first year for my first year of master's degree in computer science at Paris-Saclay University.
